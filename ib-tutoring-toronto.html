IB Chemistry

IB Chemistry Tutoring in Toronto

IB Chemistry is separated into Standard Level (SL) and High Level (HL). Standard level is slightly more difficult than high school chemistry 11 and 12. However, High level approach the difficult of first year university. To perform in IB chemistry courses, the student needs to understand and apply the concept in a variety of difficult, situation based problems.

IB Chemistry is a challenging subject. Many students resort to memorizing formulas and hoping to plug in a number to receive the answer - and do well on tests and exams. Oftentimes the teacher will change the question - especially on application problems, where the student will get student, result in poor grades.

Memorizing steps and equations doesn't work in IB chemistry. You need to start with the foundation (such as stoichiometry, or calculating equilibrium). The. next step is to understand the concept and know how to apply to specific problems on IB chemistry. Then the next step is to go through a variety of application problems, which is especially difficult in IB chemistry. Then learn important problem solving skills tailored to IB chemistry. These are all situation based questions that you will encounter on your IB chemistry tests and exams

IB Physics Tutoring

IB Physics Tutoring in Toronto

IB Physics is separated into Standard Level (SL) and Higher Level (HL). Standard level is slightly more difficult than high school physics 11 and 12. However, Higher level approach the difficult of first year university. To perform in IB physics courses, the student needs to understand the concept and sharpen his or her problem solving skills, which is essential. There are units such as electromagnetism that is very abstract and difficult to comprehend. Same for diffraction grating.

IB Physics is a challenging subject for a lot of students. The key challenge is that IB Physics is problem solving based. Students are accustomed to math, where they will have a formula and "plug-in" the number to derive the answer, following the procedures and steps. For IB Physics, you need to know which formula to use in the first place and how to manipulate the formula according to the situation.

Memorizing steps and equations doesn't work in IB Physics. All questions in IB physics are application based, they are word problems where the solution changes along with the situation. The teacher can change the question slight, such as adding friction or an extra box, your entire solution - the equation you use, the givens, the approach, will be different. Understanding the concepts and apply them, instead of memorizing equations, is the key to perform well in IB Physics.

IB Math Tutoring

IB Math tutoring in Toronto

IB Math is separated into Standard Level (SL) and Higher Level (HL). Standard level is slightly more difficult than high school math, such as advanced functions and calculus. However, Higher level math approach the difficulty of first year university. Beside the limits and derivatives, students need to learn integration at first year level of difficulty. To perform in IB math courses, the student needs to understand the concepts and connect these concepts, and know when to apply which one in deciphering the problem.

IB Math is a challenging course for a lot of students. The key challenge is that IB Math cover a variety of topics and they are all intricately connected. Therefore, students need to have a deep understanding of the materials and know how to apply them. Secondly it depends a solid foundation on the student's part. Math is cumulative, therefore the student at IB Math must master grade 9-11 math. The first two steps of a solution is typically the new materials. However, all the subsequent steps, such as simplifying the answer is all based on foundation skills. Students lacking foundation skills will lose a significant portion of the marks.

Memorizing steps and equations doesn't work in IB Math. Most questions in IB Math are application based. Different scenario request adjustment on the equations and the solution is often time very different. Understanding the concepts and knowing when to apply what is really the key to succeed in IB Math
